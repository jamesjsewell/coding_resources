A small polished app is better than a complex mess

I would work on things that i could see myself working on for the next 2 years. Making the personal projects I create matter to me is important. If my projects don't matter to me, it will show and they won't matter to others. To achieve creating work that i am excited about, it is essential to not let expectations and perfectionism get in the way.

I would think of the project in versions. The first version I create is the prototype The next version is the minimum viable product or “mvp”, then “alpha”, “beta”, and so on.

What constitutes a “version” does each new change make the app a new version? No, I think of each version as its own complete app. I’ll know that the version is complete when it functions. That way when someone gives me feedback I can save it for a later version. Why bother doing things this way? Well as I am just starting out it is best that I present projects that I am proud of and am not stressing over. This will make me more confident and excited about presenting and talking about my work.

How do I keep track of versions? Being proficient with GitHub or something similar is great, this will be the optimal way to approach it. If I were beginning to learn GitHub and weren’t yet proficient with it, I would use Google drive or some kind of storage system to store the versions of my app. Make each version “good enough”. By good enough I mean I have to lower my expectations of the scope of the project and always keep in mind that a new feature can be introduced in later versions. I would do my best to first of all make the simple app work. Then work on making the version presentable.
